O Driscoll Clan Gathering Events

The O Driscoll Clan Gathering committee at Baltimore organise the mid-summer programme of events for the annual international O Driscoll Clan Gathering. It is a three day Friday to late Sunday event over the last weekend in the month of June. Activities take place in and around the ancestral home area of the O Driscolls, the Village of Baltimore and the offshore islands of Cape Clear, Heir and Sherkin in West County Cork, Ireland.

The annual programme of events includes a reception where people first get to know others in the international O Driscoll family; There is the Signing of the O Driscoll Register and a short talk on some aspect of O Driscoll heritage in Baltimore Castle; There could perhaps, be a book launch or an exhibition; story-telling and singing sessions. Visits to historic sites and the ruins of one time O Driscoll castles and other places associated with O Driscolls of other days all regularly feature on the weekend programme.

Early arriving visitors may choose to take an afternoon or evening sightseeing boat trip around Baltimore Harbour or take the trip to Fastnet Rock with its magnificent Fastnet Lighthouse. Music sessions in pubs and entertainment take place each evening in the Village Square underneath the beautifully restored Dún na Séad, Baltimore Castle that was for centuries, the home of the O Driscoll Clan Chieftains.

While attending The Clan Gathering, O Driscoll visitors are at home in West Cork where the folk memory of times past and family genealogy is still very much alive. Being in the company of so many other O Driscolls and in their ancestral home place is often the springboard for many to go in search of their roots. The O Driscoll Memorial Archive at Cape Clear island is dedicated to the collection and archiving of documentation relating to members of the worldwide O Driscoll family. The Skibbereen Heritage Centre and Cork County Library, Local Studies Unit will always provide assistance to people in search of their O Driscoll roots. Talking about your Driscoll ancestry to the locals in any part of West Cork will usually bring a friendly and informative response.

Thursday

On the Thursday afternoon and evening of the last weekend in June every year, O Driscoll visitors start arriving in Baltimore Village, Co. Cork, Ireland. They may wish to take a boat trip around Baltimore Harbour, returning at sunset. Music sessions follow in local pubs, where old friends meet and newcomers traditionally introduce themselves to lots of other O Driscolls.

Saturday

A day trip to Cape Clear Island takes place on Saturday when the Clan members have made their way by 10:30 am to Baltimore Pier to take the Cape Clear Island Ferry for the 45 minute trip to the island. On arrival, people can make their way to the museum on foot or by shuttle buses where at noon the O Driscoll Memorial Archive holds the Annual O Driscoll Remembrance Ceremony. Details of the O Driscoll Remembrance Ceremony and more O Driscoll Events are to be found Click here.

After visiting the museum and viewing some of the O Driscoll related exhibition material and O Driscoll Archive Collection, there will be lunches, tea, coffee etc. available at bars near the North Harbour. Visits to historic and other sites may be organised in the afternoon. A music session, perhaps a quiz or a visit to historic and archaeological sites or other activities may take place on the day. At 4:30 pm, the ferry leaves Cape Clear for Baltimore. On Saturday night, O Driscoll Clan members usually spend some time chatting with new friends, making contacts and perhaps discussing their common O Driscoll genealogy and heritage. It is also a time for engaging with the local community and relaxing in comfort at local restaurants and pubs.

Sunday

A day trip by ferry to Cape Clear island takes place on Saturday when the Clan members have made their way by 10.30 am to Baltimore Pier to take the Cape Clear Island Ferry for the 40 minute journey to the island. On arrival people can make their way to the Island Museum on foot or by shuttle bus where at noon The O Driscoll Memorial Archive holds the Annual O Driscoll Remembrance Ceremony. For details, Click here.

Sherkin Island Franciscan Priory established by Fineen Ó Driscoll in 1640

By 3 pm Sunday afternoon, O Driscolls will have crowded into Baltimore Square in the shadow of the O Driscoll Castle, Dún na Séad, the pride of all their castles. The name of a new O Driscoll Chieftain is announced every two years and there is the formal handing over of the O Driscoll Chieftains Chain of Office. Great excitement and music follow for a number of hours. Most of the visitors from overseas spend Sunday night gathered together in the village, and next day may perhaps continue with their holiday arrangements around County Cork, Kerry and elsewhere.
